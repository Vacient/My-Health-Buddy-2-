Health & Safety Alert ‚Äî Mental & Physical Health

Clear warning signs, practical steps you can take right now, and trusted resources. If you are in immediate danger, call local emergency services.

Immediate warning signs

Mental health: feeling hopeless, thinking about self-harm, extreme mood changes, talking about death. Treat these as urgent.

Physical health: chest pain, difficulty breathing, sudden numbness/weakness, severe bleeding, loss of consciousness ‚Äî seek emergency care.

Get help now

What to do in the next 30 minutes

  • Find a safe, calm space. If unsafe at home, go to a public place or call emergency services.
  • Tell someone you trust exactly how you feel ‚Äî call or message them now.
  • Follow your support plan, or use the resources panel or a crisis line immediately.
  • For physical emergencies (chest pain, severe bleeding), call emergency number at once.
Warning: If you are thinking about harming yourself or others, or you are in immediate danger, contact emergency services right now.

Safety steps you can take

For mental distress

  • Delay ‚Äî give yourself 10 minutes and do a grounding exercise (5 things you see, 4 you touch, 3 you hear).
  • Remove immediate means (medication, sharp objects) if safe.
  • Use breathing: 4s inhale ‚Äî 4s hold ‚Äî 6s exhale for 2‚Äì3 minutes.
  • Contact a trusted friend, family member, or crisis line.

For physical emergency

  • Call emergency services immediately for life-threatening signs.
  • If trained, provide basic first aid (CPR, stop bleeding).
  • Keep the person warm and monitored; do not give anything by mouth if unconscious.

When to seek professional help

See a mental health professional if you notice persistent sadness, loss of interest in daily life, severe anxiety, changes in sleep/appetite, or thoughts of self-harm. For severe unexplained physical symptoms, see a medical professional.

Prepare a quick safety plan

  1. Name three people you can call: Person 1, Person 2, Person 3.
  2. List coping strategies: breathe, walk, music, grounding, distraction tasks.
  3. Write emergency contacts and your local emergency number somewhere visible.
